About Us

Intel Corporation is an American multinational corporation and one of the world's largest and highest-valued semiconductor chip manufacturers. Founded on July 18, 1968, by Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore, Intel has its headquarters in Santa Clara, California.

  1. Industry Leadership: Intel is a dominant player in the semiconductor industry, particularly in the design and manufacturing of microprocessors, system-on-chip (SoC) products, and other integrated circuits. It is renowned for its innovation and cutting-edge technology in the field of computing.
  2. Products: Intel's product range includes microprocessors for desktops, laptops, servers, and embedded systems, as well as memory and storage solutions, networking products, and other semiconductor-based technologies. Notable product lines include the Intel Core series of processors, Xeon processors for servers and workstations, and Intel Optane memory and storage products.
  3. Market Presence: Intel has a significant global presence, with operations in over 50 countries and a diverse customer base spanning various industries, including technology, automotive, healthcare, and telecommunications. It sells its products through direct sales, distributors, and OEM partners.
  4. Competition: Intel faces stiff competition from other semiconductor companies, including AMD, NVIDIA, Qualcomm, and TSMC, among others. The semiconductor industry is highly competitive, with rapid technological advancements and evolving market dynamics.
